Who Created Python?!

    Python was developed and released by Dutch programmer Gudio van Rossum, with the main goal of it's creation being to stave off his boredom during the Christmas holidays, a time when his work offices would be closed.

    Originally created in 1989, this passion project was started by Van Rossum in the hopes of marketing it as a successor to ABC. Over the years Python was slowly built on as a language focused around two ideals:

    1. Simplicity

    2. Being open-source

    It wasn't until 1989 that Van Rossum secured funding for Python from the Defense Advanced Resarch Projects Agency of the United States government.



Who Is Guido van Rossum?

    Guido van Rossum, born January 31st, 1956, is a renowned Programmer originally known for his work developing the ABC Programming language in January of 1987 at the Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica. Even to this day, Van Rossum cherishes his time spent on the project, calling it the sole reason for Python's creation, as he states...

    "I try to mention ABC's influence because I'm indebted to everything I learned during that project and to the people who worked on it."

    -Guido van Rossum - Venners, Bill. "The Making of Python". www.artima.com. Archived from the original on 1 September 2016. Retrieved 14 September 2016.

    What Are His Accomplishments?

    Guido van Rossum is known for many things outside of just the creation of Python, this list includes but is not limited to:

    1. Working for Google between 2005-2012, where he developed Mondarian, a web-based code review system written in Python and still used within the company.

    2. While working at the Dutch institution Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica, Van Rossum contributed a vital Glob() routine to BSD Unix.

    3. Van Rossum was also declared Benevolent Dictator For Life over the Python language, and has been documented as the original reason for the title's creation.

      As a BDFL he held say over any final decision on disputes within the open-source software development community. Guido van Rossum stepped down from this title in July of 2018.

    Awards and Accolades:

    • During the 2002 FOSDEM Conference in Brussels, the Free Software Foundation awarded Van Rossum the 2001 Award for the Advancement of Free Software for his work on Python.

    • He was awarded a NLUUG award in May of 2003

    • In 2006 the Assoication for Computing Machinery recognized Van Rossum as a Distingusihed Engineer

    • In 2018 the Computer History Museum awarded him with the honor of Fellowship

    • In 2019, the Cenntrum Wiskunde & Informatica awarded Guido van Rossum with one of the first ever Dijkstra Fellowships, an awarded given to those who have made exceptional contributions to the field of computer science.
